3.08 Disabling Condition


This is the first and last mention of Disabling Condition... All notes related to reporting on chronic homelessness assume that it is 'Yes'.

)

The current version of 3.917A for ES, SH, and SO projects

Type of Residence and Length of Stay are required for all project types -- in 3.917A and 3.917B. 3.917A also requires the "chronic homelessness" fields -- Approximate date..., Number of times..., and Number of months... -- regardless of whether or not the Type of Residence and Length of Stay are inconsistent with chronic homelessness.

This leads to bad data. There are ways to resolve the discrepancies in reporting, but they aren't consistent.

APR CH Reporting:

Regardless of Type of Residence and/or Length of Stay for 3.917A, a person is counted as chronically homeless if:

  • Approximate date... is 365+ days prior to EntryDate; or
  • Number of times is four or more and Number of months... is 12 or more.

LSA CH Reporting

‚ùå Not Chronically Homeless

The LSA only considers the Approximate Date..., Number of times..., and Number of months... if:

  • Type of Residence is ES, SH, or Street; or
  • The client was on the street or in ES/SH immediately prior to an institutional stay of less than 90 days or a stay of less than 7 days anywhere else.

3.917B solves this problem -- but creates another*

*along with some annoyance.

3.917B only collects the CH fields if Type of Residence and length of stay indicate that the client might be chronically homeless. This is good!

BUT! -- notice that "length of stay" above doesn't look like a field name should look... That's not a typo -- 3.917B ignores the existing Length of Stay field, creates two (‚Åâ) entirely redundant fields, and sets up another potential data discrepancy.

Proposed Solution

  • Use a single data element. It's simpler -- and the same circumstances will result in the same data regardless of project type.
  • Ask the chronic homelessness questions only if there's a chance the client is actually CH based on living situation and length of stay (like 3.917B).
  • BUT! Use the Length of Stay field that end users have to answer anyway -- get rid of "Did you stay less than 90 days?" and "Did you stay less than 7 nights?"
